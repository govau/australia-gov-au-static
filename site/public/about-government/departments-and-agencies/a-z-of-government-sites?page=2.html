A–Z of government sites

Attorney-General's Department

Delivers programs and policies to maintain and improve Australia's law and justice framework and strengthen our national security and emergency management.

Attorney-General's Department

Auditing and Assurance Standards Board

An independent, statutory agency responsible for developing standards and guidance for auditors and providers of other assurance services.

Auditing and Assurance Standards Board

AusTender – The Australian Government Tender System

Provides centralised publication of Australian Government business opportunities, annual procurement plans, multi-use lists and contracts awarded. You must be a registered user to download tender documents, receive business opportunities and addenda notifications and lodge tenders.

Department of Finance

Austrade – Australian Trade and Investment Commission

Assists Australian businesses to expand their business outside Australia with information about export markets, grants and assistance and promotes and supports productive foreign investment and international education in Australia.

More

  • Exporters
  • Buyers
  • Investors
  • Education

Australian Trade and Investment Commission

Australasian Antarctic Expedition and Mawson's Huts Historic Site

Provides a history of the Australasian Antarctic Expedition 1911–1914 and information on the Mawson's Huts Historic Site in Antarctica.

Australian Antarctic Division

Australasian Court Administrators Group

Comprises the chief executives responsible for the administration of the courts and major tribunals of Australia and New Zealand. To use this site you must be an authorized user with a valid Username and Password.

High Court of Australia

Australasian Digital Recordkeeping Initiative

Formed to promote a common approach to digital recordkeeping and to collaborate and share resources in the development of practical strategies for enabling the creation and preservation of, and long-term access to, born-digital and digitised records.

National Archives of Australia

Australasian Procurement and Construction Council

The peak council whose 11 member agencies are responsible for procurement, construction and asset management policy for the Australian, state and territory governments. Papua New Guinea is an associate member.

Australasian Procurement and Construction Council Inc.

Australia and the Vietnam War

Provides resources on Australia's involvement in the Vietnam War. It was the longest twentieth century conflict in which Australians have participated, lasting from 1962 to 1972 and involved some 60,000 personnel.

Board of Studies NSW

Australia Awards

International scholarships and fellowships offering the next generation of global leaders an opportunity to undertake study, research and professional development in Australia and for high-achieving Australians to do the same overseas. They are awarded each year to citizens of participating countries from Asia, the Pacific, Middle East and Africa.

Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade

Australia Council for the Arts

The Australian Government's arts funding and advisory body. It invests in artistic excellence through support for all facets of the creative process, and is committed to the arts being accessible to all Australians.

Australia Council for the Arts

Australia Day

Australia Day, January 26, is the biggest day of celebration in the country and is observed as a public holiday in all states and territories. People come together as a nation to celebrate what's great about Australia and being Australian.

More

  • History, ceremonies, reconciliation

National Australia Day Council

Australia Global Alumni

A virtual global network for international scholars and fellows who have studied in Australia and for Australians who have studied overseas.  

Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade

Australia Minerals

Portal site linking to geoscience expertise and research support enabling investors to tap into Australia's diverse geological potential in a range of commodities to achieve one of the highest exploration returns.

Geoscience Australia

Australia New Zealand Policing Advisory Agency (ANZPAA)

Partners with Australian and New Zealand policing agencies to identify opportunities for improved performance that results in better community safety outcomes in Australia and New Zealand.

Australia New Zealand Policing Advisory Agency

Australia Plus TV

Provides Australian television and radio content to audiences in the Asia Pacific region.

Australian Broadcasting Corporation

Australia Post

Provides high-quality mail and parcel services to all Australians. It is a Government Business Enterprise (GBE) and is the major organisation in the Australian postal industry.

Australian Postal Corporation

Australia Telescope National Facility

CSIRO's radio astronomy observatories (known as the ATNF) support Australia’s research in radio astronomy and can be used by researchers from institutions all over the world.

CSIRO, the Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation

Australia Telescope Outreach and Education

Provides the media and general public with information about the CSIRO's radio astronomy observatories and educational resources on aspects of astronomy.

CSIRO, the Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation

Australia Unlimited

Shares the stories of Australia's innovators, creators and entrepreneurs and promotes the intellectual, commercial and creative credentials of contemporary Australia to the world.

Australian Trade and Investment Commission

Australia's aid program

Promotes Australia’s national interests by contributing to sustainable economic growth and poverty reduction.

Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade

Australia's Chief Scientist

Dr Alan Finkel AO, as Australia's Chief Scientist, provides high-level independent advice to the Government on science, technology and innovation issues of central or emerging importance.

Department of Industry, Innovation and Science

Australia's Future Tax System

The Henry review examined Australian and state government taxes and made recommendations. Includes the Final Report.

Australia's involvement in the Korean War

Provides resources on Australia's involvement in the Korean War. Covers the major developments of the war, the war in the air and at sea, the role of Australian nurses and has special features such as animated maps of the key battles of the war (the Battle of Kapyong and the Battle of Han River).

Board of Studies NSW

Australia's Prime Ministers

Provides information about the life and times of Australia's prime ministers from 1901 to the present.

National Archives of Australia
