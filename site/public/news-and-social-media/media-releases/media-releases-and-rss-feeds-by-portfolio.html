What are RSS feeds

RSS is a file format that is used to let people know when a certain website or part of a website (for example latest news areas and blogs) has been updated with new content.

The service which alerts users to these updates is known as an RSS feed.

For more information on RSS feeds and podcasts and how to use them see RSS feeds and podcasts.

Media releases and RSS feeds by portfolio

A portfolio is a minister's area of responsibility as a member of Cabinet. As a general rule, there is one department in each portfolio.

Each government department has its own departmental website with ministers and parliamentary secretaries often having their own websites and RSS feeds as well. Within each portfolio there are also numerous agency and program websites and RSS media feeds.
